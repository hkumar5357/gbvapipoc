The Legal Minefield

44 states. Different laws.
One wrong step.

Mandatory reporting obligations, accommodation requirements, protective order procedures, and retaliation protections vary dramatically by state. What's required in California is different from Texas — and neither is optional.

Mandatory Reporting
18 states require employers to report certain workplace violence incidents. The trigger thresholds differ by state.
Accommodation Requirements
27 states require employers to provide schedule or location accommodations for employees affected by domestic violence.
Protection Order Support
Employers in 34 states have specific obligations when an employee obtains a restraining order that affects the workplace.
Retaliation Liability
Taking adverse employment action after a disclosure — even unintentionally — creates retaliation liability in all 50 states.
